🔑 Recall — Cumulative Frequency & Box Plots

  • Cumulative frequency is a running total of frequencies.
  • Median position: $\frac{n}{2}$ on the cumulative frequency axis; read across to the curve, then down to the $x$-axis.
  • Lower quartile (Q1) position: $\frac{n}{4}$; same method as median.
  • Upper quartile (Q3) position: $\frac{3n}{4}$; same method as median.
  • IQR = Q3 − Q1
  • Box plot shows: minimum, Q1, median, Q3, maximum. Whiskers extend to min/max unless there are outliers.

📘 Worked Example

A food scientist measures the weights of 80 potatoes. The cumulative frequency curve has the following key points:

Weight (g)50100150200250300
Cumulative frequency52242627480

Step 1 — Find the median:

Position = $\frac{80}{2} = 40$ → Read from CF = 40 across to curve, down to 140 g

Step 2 — Find Q1 and Q3:

Q1 position = $\frac{80}{4} = 20$ → 95 g

Q3 position = $\frac{3 \times 80}{4} = 60$ → 195 g

IQR = $195 - 95 = 100$ g

Step 3 — Draw the box plot:

Min = 20 g, Max = 400 g (given). Box plot scale from 0 to 400:

Box plot showing potato weights (g). The yellow line is the median.

Question 2 — Medium [4 marks]

M1 — for recognizing $760 - 420 = 340$
(a) 340 potatoes weigh more than 200 g A1
A1 — Median = 190 g (accept 185–195)
(b) Q1 = 135–140 g, Q3 = 242–247 g, IQR = 102–112 g A1

Question 3 — Exam Style [5 marks]

M1 — for $200 - 100 = 100$ or equivalent
(a) 100 apples weigh more than 150 g A1
A1 — Median = 160 g, Q1 = 120 g, Q3 = 200 g
(b) IQR = 80 g A1
M1A1 — Box plot with box and whiskers; M1 for structure, A1 for all statistics in correct positions

Question 4 — Challenging [4 marks]

(a) IQR = $80 - 35 = 45$ A1
(b) Lower fence = $35 - 1.5(45) = 35 - 67.5 = -32.5$
Upper fence = $80 + 1.5(45) = 80 + 67.5 = 147.5$
Min = 15 > −32.5, so not an outlier. Max = 180 > 147.5, so is an outlier. M1A1
(c) Box plot with whisker to ~147.5 and max as isolated point at 180 A1
