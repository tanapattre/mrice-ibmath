Scaffolded Practice: Sampling Methods

IB DP Mathematics: AI SL — Probability & Statistics · Syllabus 4.7

🔑 Recall — Sampling Methods

  • Stratified sampling: Population divided into subgroups (strata); sample proportionally from each.
  • Quota sampling: Non-random; interviewer selects sample to match population proportions.
  • Convenience sampling: Selecting whoever is easiest to reach (e.g., first volunteers).
  • Systematic sampling: Selecting every $k$th member from a list.
  • Simple random sampling: Every member has equal chance; e.g., random number generator.

📘 Worked Example

A school has 850 students across 4 grade levels:

Grade9101112
Students180210230230

A stratified sample of 30 students is needed.

Step 1 — Calculate the proportion for each grade:

Grade 9: $\frac{180}{850} \times 30 = 6.35$ → 6 students

Grade 10: $\frac{210}{850} \times 30 = 7.41$ → 7 students

Grade 11: $\frac{230}{850} \times 30 = 8.12$ → 8 students

Grade 12: $\frac{230}{850} \times 30 = 8.12$ → 9 students

Check: $6 + 7 + 8 + 9 = 30$ ✓

Step 2 — Identify the method:

The principal stands at the cafeteria door and asks the first 20 students who walk by to participate.

This is quota sampling (trying to match proportions) and convenience sampling (choosing whoever is easiest to reach).

Question 3 — Exam Style[5 marks]

Grade7891011
Students120140160180140

The Principal wants a sample of 25 students where the proportion from each grade matches the school as closely as possible.

(a) Calculate the number of Grade 10 students who should be in the sample. (3 marks)

(b) The Principal selects students by asking those who took part in a previous survey if they would like to take part again. She takes the first who reply positively, up to the maximum needed. State which two sampling methods best describe this. (2 marks)

Question 4 — Challenging[3 marks]

(a) Identify the sampling method used. (1 mark)

(b) Compare and contrast this method with systematic sampling. (2 marks)
