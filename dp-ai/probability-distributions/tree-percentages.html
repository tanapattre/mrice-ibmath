🔑 Recall — Tree Diagrams with Percentages

  • Tree diagrams with percentages: Branches show probabilities as decimals or percentages.
  • $\P(A \cap B)$: $= \P(A) \times \P(B|A)$ — multiply along branches.
  • Law of Total Probability: $\P(B) = \P(A \cap B) + \P(A' \cap B)$ — add different paths.
  • Conditional probability: $\displaystyle \P(A|B) = \frac{\P(A \cap B)}{\P(B)}$
  • Complement: $\P(A') = 1 - \P(A)$

📘 Worked Example

In a school, 40% of students play sport. Of those who play sport, 70% pass math. Of those who don't play sport, 50% pass math.

Step 1 — Draw the tree diagram:

Step 2 — $\P(\text{Sport} \cap \text{Pass})$:

$\P(\text{Sport} \cap \text{Pass}) = 0.40 \times 0.70 = 0.28$

Step 3 — $\P(\text{No Sport} \cap \text{Pass})$:

$\P(\text{No Sport} \cap \text{Pass}) = 0.60 \times 0.50 = 0.30$

Step 4 — $\P(\text{Pass})$:

$\P(\text{Pass}) = 0.28 + 0.30 = 0.58$

Step 5 — $\P(\text{Sport} | \text{Pass})$:

$\P(\text{Sport} | \text{Pass}) = \frac{0.28}{0.58} = 0.483$ (3 sf)

Question 1 — Basic[4 marks]

In a city, 25% of people own a car. Of car owners, 80% have a driving license. Of non-owners, 60% have a driving license.

(a) Complete the tree diagram. (2 marks)

(b) Find $\P(\text{license})$. (2 marks)

Question 2 — Medium[5 marks]

In a town, 45% of households own a dog. Of dog owners, 90% have their dog vaccinated. It is known that 72% of all households have a vaccinated dog.

(a) Complete the tree diagram. (1 mark)

(b) Find $\P(\text{vaccinated} | \text{not dog owner})$. (2 marks)

(c) A household is chosen at random and has a vaccinated dog. Find the probability they do not own a dog. (2 marks)

Question 3 — Exam Style[6 marks]

In a country, 28% of adults travel abroad each year. Of those who travel, 65% speak two or more languages. It is known that 52% of all adults speak two or more languages.

(a) Find $\P(\text{speaks 2+ languages} | \text{does not travel})$. (3 marks)

(b) Given that an adult speaks two or more languages, find the probability they travel abroad. (3 marks)

Question 4 — Challenging[6 marks]

A medical test for a disease has the following properties:

  • $\P(\text{disease}) = 0.02$
  • $\P(\text{positive} | \text{disease}) = 0.95$
  • $\P(\text{positive} | \text{no disease}) = 0.08$

(a) Find $\P(\text{positive})$. (2 marks)

(b) Given a positive test result, find $\P(\text{disease} | \text{positive})$. (2 marks)

(c) Given a negative test result, find $\P(\text{no disease} | \text{negative})$. (2 marks)
