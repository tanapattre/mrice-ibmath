IB DP Mathematics: AI SL — Probability & Statistics · Syllabus 4.5

🔑 Recall — Tree Diagrams & Conditional Probability

  • Tree diagram: First branch = first event; second branch = second event.
  • Without replacement: Second branch probabilities change after the first draw.
  • $\P(A \text{ and } B)$: Multiply along branches. $\P(A) \times \P(B|A)$
  • $\P(\text{same colour})$: Add different paths. $\P(BB) + P(B_k B_k)$
  • Conditional probability: $\displaystyle \P(A|B) = \frac{\P(A \text{ and } B)}{\P(B)}$

📘 Worked Example

A bag contains 2 red and 3 blue counters. Two counters are drawn without replacement.

Step 1 — First draw:

$\P(\text{Red}) = \frac{2}{5}$    $\P(\text{Blue}) = \frac{3}{5}$

Step 2 — Second draw (if Red first):

$\P(\text{Red}) = \frac{1}{4}$    $\P(\text{Blue}) = \frac{3}{4}$

Step 3 — Second draw (if Blue first):

$\P(\text{Red}) = \frac{2}{4}$    $\P(\text{Blue}) = \frac{2}{4}$

Step 4 — Complete tree diagram:

                    [Start]
                   /       \
              2/5 /         \ 3/5
                 /           \
              [Red]        [Blue]
              /   \          /   \
         1/4 /     \ 3/4 2/4 /     \ 2/4
            /       \      /       \
        [Red]   [Blue] [Red]   [Blue]
         RR      RB     BR      BB
       2/20    6/20   6/20    6/20

Step 5 — $\P(\text{both same colour})$:

$\P(RR) + \P(BB) = \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{1}{4} + \frac{3}{5} \times \frac{2}{4} = \frac{2}{20} + \frac{6}{20} = \frac{8}{20} = \frac{2}{5}$

Step 6 — $\P(\text{Red} | \text{same colour})$:

$\P(\text{Red} | \text{same}) = \frac{\P(RR)}{\P(\text{same})} = \frac{2/20}{8/20} = \frac{2}{8} = \frac{1}{4}$

Question 1 — Basic[3 marks]

A bag contains 4 green and 2 yellow marbles. Two marbles are drawn without replacement.

(a) Complete the tree diagram. (1 mark)

(b) Find $\P(\text{both green})$. (2 marks)

Question 2 — Medium[5 marks]

A drawer contains 5 black and 3 white socks. Two socks are taken without replacement.

(a) Complete the tree diagram. (1 mark)

(b) Find the probability that both socks are the same colour. (2 marks)

(c) Given that both socks are the same colour, find the probability that they are both black. (2 marks)

Question 3 — Exam Style[6 marks]

A class has 6 boys and 4 girls. Two students are chosen without replacement for a team.

(a) Complete the tree diagram. (1 mark)

(b) Find $\P(\text{at least one girl})$. (2 marks)

(c) Given that at least one girl is chosen, find the probability that both are girls. (3 marks)

Question 4 — Challenging[5 marks]

A deck of cards has 12 face cards and 40 non-face cards. Two cards are drawn without replacement.

(a) Find $\P(\text{at least one face card})$. (2 marks)

(b) Given that at least one face card is drawn, find $\P(\text{both face cards})$. (3 marks)

Question 1 — Basic [3 marks]

A1 — for correct tree: First draw G=4/6, Y=2/6; Second draw (if G) G=3/5, Y=2/5; (if Y) G=4/5, Y=1/5
(b) $\P(\text{both green}) = \frac{4}{6} \times \frac{3}{5} = \frac{12}{30} = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$ M1A1

Question 2 — Medium [5 marks]

A1 — for correct tree structure with all probabilities
(b) $\P(\text{same}) = \frac{5}{8} \times \frac{4}{7} + \frac{3}{8} \times \frac{2}{7} = \frac{20}{56} + \frac{6}{56} = \frac{26}{56} = \frac{13}{28} \approx 0.464$ M1A1
(c) $\P(\text{black} | \text{same}) = \frac{20/56}{26/56} = \frac{20}{26} = \frac{10}{13} \approx 0.769$ M1A1A1
Note: Award M1 for correct conditional probability formula setup.

Question 3 — Exam Style [6 marks]

A1 — for correct tree with all probabilities
(b) $\P(\text{at least one girl}) = 1 - \P(\text{both boys}) = 1 - \frac{6}{10} \times \frac{5}{9} = 1 - \frac{30}{90} = \frac{60}{90} = \frac{2}{3} \approx 0.667$ M1A1
(c) $\P(\text{both girls} | \text{at least one girl}) = \frac{\P(\text{both girls})}{\P(\text{at least one girl})} = \frac{4/10 \times 3/9}{2/3} = \frac{12/90}{60/90} = \frac{12}{60} = \frac{1}{5} = 0.2$ M1A1A1

Question 4 — Challenging [5 marks]

(a) $\P(\text{at least one face}) = 1 - \P(\text{both non-face}) = 1 - \frac{40}{52} \times \frac{39}{51} = 1 - \frac{1560}{2652} = \frac{1092}{2652} = \frac{91}{221} \approx 0.412$ M1A1
(b) $\P(\text{both face} | \text{at least one face}) = \frac{12/52 \times 11/51}{1092/2652} = \frac{132/2652}{1092/2652} = \frac{132}{1092} = \frac{11}{91} \approx 0.121$ M1A1A1
