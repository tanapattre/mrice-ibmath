Final Exam Review 2 — Number & Algebra

DP AI SL · 30 marks · Suggested time: 40 minutes

⏱ Full marks may not be awarded where working is unclear or incomplete. Where appropriate, answers should be given to 3 significant figures unless stated otherwise. Graphic display calculators are permitted.
Question 1 [6 marks]

Riku saves 30 000 Japanese yen (JPY) in a bank account which pays a nominal annual interest rate of 3.6% compounded monthly.

(a) Calculate the value of Riku's investment after three years. Give your answer correct to two decimal places. [3]

After the three-year period, Riku withdraws 10 000 JPY from his account to buy a musical instrument. The value of the instrument depreciates at a rate of 12% per year. The value of the instrument will be 3500 JPY after t years.

(b) Calculate the value of t. [3]

Question 2 [6 marks]

In this question, give your answers to the nearest whole number.

A student in London orders a gift online from a store in Switzerland. At the checkout page, the student sees the following currency information.

1 CHF = 0.92 GBP

This means the store would receive payment in Swiss francs (CHF) and convert it to British pounds (GBP) at the stated rate. No commission is charged.

The price of the gift is 180 CHF.

(a) Calculate the cost of the gift in GBP. [3]

The student also buys a second gift from a store in Norway priced at 450 Norwegian krone (NOK). The exchange rate is 1 GBP = 13.5 NOK.

(b) Calculate the total amount the student pays in GBP for both gifts. [3]

Question 3 [6 marks]

The loudness of sound, L, measured in decibels (dB), can be modelled as a function of its intensity, I, in watts per square metre (W m−2), relative to a reference intensity I0 = 10−12 W m−2, as shown by the following equation.

$$L = 10\log_{10}\left(\frac{I}{I_0}\right)$$

A rock concert has an intensity of 0.05 W m−2.

(a) Calculate the loudness of the rock concert in decibels. [2]

A library has a loudness of 35 dB.

(b) Calculate the intensity of sound in the library. [2]

(c) Calculate how many times more intense the sound at the rock concert is compared to the sound in the library. [2]

Question 4 [12 marks]

Part A — Arithmetic Sequence

On her first day of training, a cyclist rides u1 kilometres. The distance she rides increases by the same amount, d, each day. On the seventh day, she rides 18.4 km, and on the twelfth day she rides 28.4 km.

(a.i) Write down an equation, in terms of u1 and d, for the distance she rides on the seventh day. [1]

(a.ii) Write down an equation, in terms of u1 and d, for the distance she rides on the twelfth day. [1]

(b) Calculate the value of u1 and the value of d. [2]

The cyclist trains for 25 days.

(c) Calculate the total distance, in kilometres, that she rides during the 25 days. [3]

Part B — Geometric Sequence

A new social media post receives 5000 views on its first day. The number of views the post receives decreases by 30% each day.

(d.i) Calculate the number of views the post receives on the fourth day. [3]

(d.ii) The daily number of views will first be less than 200 on the kth day. Calculate the value of k. [3]

(d.iii) Calculate the total number of views the post receives during the first 7 days. [2]

Final Exam Review 2 — Number & Algebra

Markscheme · 30 marks

Question 1 — Compound Interest & Depreciation [6 marks]

(a) $FV = 30000\left(1 + \frac{0.036}{12}\right)^{12 \times 3}$ (M1)(A1)
= 30000 × 1.11347... = 33 404.20 JPY A1
(b) $10000 \times (0.88)^t = 3500$ M1
$(0.88)^t = 0.35$
$t = \frac{\ln 0.35}{\ln 0.88} = 8.38...$ A1
$t = 8.38$ years (accept 8.39 or 8.4) A1

Question 2 — Currency Exchange [6 marks]

(a) $180 \times 0.92 = 165.60$ GBP M1A1
= 166 GBP (nearest whole number) A1
(b) Norwegian gift: $450 \div 13.5 = 33.333...$ GBP M1A1
Total = $165.60 + 33.33 = 198.93$ → 199 GBP (nearest whole number) A1

Question 3 — Logarithms [6 marks]

(a) $L = 10\log_{10}\left(\frac{0.05}{10^{-12}}\right) = 10\log_{10}(5 \times 10^{10})$ M1
$= 10 \times 10.699... = 107$ dB (accept 106.99) A1
(b) $35 = 10\log_{10}\left(\frac{I}{10^{-12}}\right)$
$3.5 = \log_{10}\left(\frac{I}{10^{-12}}\right)$ M1
$I = 10^{-12} \times 10^{3.5} = 3.16 \times 10^{-9}$ W m−2 (accept 3.162 × 10−9) A1
(c) $\frac{I_{\text{concert}}}{I_{\text{library}}} = \frac{0.05}{3.16 \times 10^{-9}} = 1.58 \times 10^{7}$ M1
≈ 15 800 000 times more intense (accept 1.58 × 107) A1

Question 4 — Sequences & Series [12 marks]

(a.i) $u_1 + 6d = 18.4$ A1
(a.ii) $u_1 + 11d = 28.4$ A1
(b) Subtracting: $5d = 10 \Rightarrow d = 2$ M1A1
$u_1 = 18.4 - 6(2) = 6.4$ km A1
(c) $S_{25} = \frac{25}{2}[2(6.4) + 24(2)] = \frac{25}{2}(12.8 + 48) = \frac{25}{2} \times 60.8 = 760$ km M1A1
OR $S_{25} = \frac{25}{2}(6.4 + 54.4) = \frac{25}{2} \times 60.8 = 760$ km A1
(d.i) $u_4 = 5000 \times (0.7)^3 = 5000 \times 0.343 = 1715$ views M1A1
(Accept 1715 or 1715.0) A1
(d.ii) $5000 \times (0.7)^{k-1} \lt 200$ M1
$(0.7)^{k-1} \lt 0.04$
$k - 1 \gt \frac{\ln 0.04}{\ln 0.7} = 9.58...$ A1
$k \gt 10.58$, so $k = 11$ A1
(d.iii) $S_7 = \frac{5000(1 - 0.7^7)}{1 - 0.7} = \frac{5000(1 - 0.0823543)}{0.3} = \frac{5000 \times 0.9176457}{0.3}$ M1
$= 15 294$ views (accept 15 300 or 15 294.1) A1
