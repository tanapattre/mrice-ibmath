Final Exam Review 4 — Probability Distributions

DP AI SL · 30 marks · Suggested time: 40 minutes

⏱ Full marks may not be awarded where working is unclear or incomplete. Where appropriate, answers should be given to 3 significant figures unless stated otherwise. Graphic display calculators are permitted.
Question 1 [8 marks]

A quality control inspector tests 12 light bulbs from a large batch. It is known that 8% of bulbs from this factory are defective. Assume each bulb is independent.

(a) Calculate the probability that exactly 2 of the 12 bulbs are defective. [2]

(b) Calculate the probability that at least 1 of the 12 bulbs is defective. [3]

(c) Calculate the expected number of defective bulbs in the sample of 12. [2]

(d) Calculate the standard deviation of the number of defective bulbs. [1]

Question 2 [8 marks]

The heights of sunflower plants in a botanical garden are normally distributed with mean 185 cm and standard deviation 22 cm.

(a) Calculate the probability that a randomly chosen plant has a height between 170 cm and 200 cm. [2]

(b) Calculate the probability that a plant is taller than 210 cm. [2]

(c) Calculate the height below which the shortest 15% of plants fall. [3]

(d) Calculate the interquartile range of the heights. [1]

Question 3 [7 marks]

A mobile phone company offers customers a choice of two data plans for the next month.

Option A: Pay a fixed fee of $30 for unlimited data.

Option B: Pay a $10 base fee plus $0.50 per MB used. The amount of data a customer uses in a month is normally distributed with mean 45 MB and standard deviation 12 MB.

(a) Calculate the expected cost of Option B. [3]

(b) Calculate the probability that a customer using Option B pays more than $30. [2]

(c) State, giving a reason, which option a customer should choose. [2]

Question 4 [7 marks]

A board game uses a spinner with four sectors labelled 1, 2, 3 and 4. The probability of the spinner landing on sector n is proportional to n.

(a) Show that $\P(4) = 0.4$. [2]

(b) Complete the following probability distribution table. [1]

Score ($x$)1234
$\P(X=x)$0.4

(c) Calculate the expected score when the spinner is spun once. [2]

(d) Calculate the variance of the score. [2]

Question 1 — Binomial Distribution [8 marks]

(a) $X \sim B(12, 0.08)$. $\P(X=2) = \binom{12}{2}(0.08)^2(0.92)^{10}$ M1
$= 66 \times 0.0064 \times 0.4344 = 0.183$ (accept 0.184) A1
(b) $\P(X \geq 1) = 1 - \P(X=0) = 1 - (0.92)^{12}$ M1A1
$= 1 - 0.3677 = 0.632$ (accept 0.633) A1
(c) $E(X) = np = 12 \times 0.08 = 0.96$ M1A1
(d) $\text{SD} = \sqrt{np(1-p)} = \sqrt{12 \times 0.08 \times 0.92} = \sqrt{0.8832} = 0.940$ A1

Question 2 — Normal Distribution [8 marks]

(a) $z_1 = \frac{170-185}{22} = -0.682$, $z_2 = \frac{200-185}{22} = 0.682$ M1
$\P(170 < X < 200) = \P(-0.682 < Z < 0.682) = 0.752 - 0.248 = 0.504$ (accept 0.503–0.505) A1
(b) $z = \frac{210-185}{22} = 1.136$ M1
$\P(X > 210) = \P(Z > 1.136) = 1 - 0.872 = 0.128$ (accept 0.127–0.129) A1
(c) $\P(X < k) = 0.15 \Rightarrow z = -1.036$ M1A1
$k = 185 + (-1.036)(22) = 185 - 22.79 = 162.2$ cm (accept 162–163) A1
(d) IQR = Q3 − Q1 = 185 + (0.674)(22) − [185 + (−0.674)(22)] = 2 × 0.674 × 22 = 29.7 cm (accept 29.6–29.8) A1

Question 3 — Normal + Expected Value [7 marks]

(a) $E(\text{cost}) = 10 + 0.5 \times E(X) = 10 + 0.5 \times 45$ M1A1
$= 10 + 22.5 = \$32.50$ A1
(b) Cost > $30 means $10 + 0.5X > 30 \Rightarrow 0.5X > 20 \Rightarrow X > 40$ M1
$z = \frac{40-45}{12} = -0.417$. $\P(X > 40) = \P(Z > -0.417) = 0.662$ (accept 0.661–0.663) A1
(c) Option A should be chosen. A1
Reason: The expected cost of Option B ($32.50) is higher than the fixed fee of Option A ($30), so on average the customer saves money with Option A. Also, there is a 66% chance of paying more than $30 with Option B. R1

Question 4 — Probability Distribution [7 marks]

(a) $\P(n) = kn$. Sum of probabilities = $k + 2k + 3k + 4k = 10k = 1$ M1
$k = 0.1$, so $\P(4) = 4k = 0.4$ AG A1
(b) $\P(1) = 0.1$, $\P(2) = 0.2$, $\P(3) = 0.3$, $\P(4) = 0.4$ A1
(c) $E(X) = 1(0.1) + 2(0.2) + 3(0.3) + 4(0.4) = 0.1 + 0.4 + 0.9 + 1.6 = 3$ M1A1
(d) $E(X^2) = 1(0.1) + 4(0.2) + 9(0.3) + 16(0.4) = 0.1 + 0.8 + 2.7 + 6.4 = 10$ M1
$\text{Var}(X) = E(X^2) - [E(X)]^2 = 10 - 9 = 1$ A1
