Question 1 [13 marks]

The diagram shows the straight line $L_1$ passing through points $A(-5, 2)$ and $B(7, 8)$.

(a) Calculate the gradient of $L_1$. [2]

$M$ is the midpoint of $AB$.

(b) Calculate the coordinates of $M$. [2]

Line $L_2$ is perpendicular to $L_1$ and passes through point $M$.

(c) Calculate the equation of $L_2$. Give your answer in the form $ax + by + d = 0$, where $a, b, d \in \mathbb{Z}$. [3]

The point $C(3, 1)$ lies on $L_2$.

(d) Verify that $C$ lies on $L_2$. [2]

(e) Calculate the distance between points $A$ and $C$. [2]

(f) Given that the length of $AB$ is $\sqrt{180}$, calculate the area of triangle $ABC$. [2]

Question 2 [6 marks]

A delivery company operates three warehouses in a city. On the following Voronoi diagram, the coordinates of the warehouses are $W_1(2, 4)$, $W_2(10, 4)$ and $W_3(6, 10)$, where distances are measured in kilometres.

The dotted lines represent the boundaries of the delivery zones for each warehouse.

(a) Write down the coordinates of the vertex where the three delivery zone boundaries meet. [1]

A new warehouse is to be built at a location that is as far as possible from the nearest existing warehouse. This new warehouse will be built within the triangle $W_1W_2W_3$.

(b) Explain why the new warehouse should be built at the vertex from part (a). [2]

The perpendicular bisector of $[W_1W_3]$ has been added to the diagram.

(c) Calculate the equation of the perpendicular bisector of $[W_2W_3]$. [3]

Question 3 [6 marks]

A rectangular garden has length $(2x + 5)$ metres and width $(x + 3)$ metres.

(a) Write down an expression for the perimeter of the garden in terms of $x$. [1]

The diagonal of the garden measures 15 metres.

(b) Using Pythagoras' theorem, show that $5x^2 + 22x - 191 = 0$. [3]

(c) Calculate the value of $x$. [2]

Question 4 [5 marks]

The diagram below shows triangle $PQR$ with $PQ = 8$ cm, $QR = 10$ cm and angle $PQR = 110°$.

(a) Calculate the length of $PR$. [2]

(b) Calculate the area of triangle $PQR$. [3]

Question 1 — Coordinate Geometry [13 marks]

(a) Gradient = $\frac{8-2}{7-(-5)} = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2}$ M1A1
(b) $M = \left(\frac{-5+7}{2}, \frac{2+8}{2}\right) = (1, 5)$ M1A1
(c) Gradient of $L_2$ = $-2$ (negative reciprocal) M1
$y - 5 = -2(x - 1) \Rightarrow y = -2x + 2 + 5 \Rightarrow y = -2x + 7$
$2x + y - 7 = 0$ (accept any integer multiple e.g. $-2x - y + 7 = 0$) A1A1
(d) Substitute $C(3, 1)$: $2(3) + (1) - 7 = 6 + 1 - 7 = 0$ AG M1A1
(e) $AC = \sqrt{(3-(-5))^2 + (1-2)^2} = \sqrt{64 + 1} = \sqrt{65} = 8.06$ M1A1
(f) Using shoelace: Area = $\frac{1}{2}|(-5)(8-1) + 7(1-2) + 3(2-8)| = \frac{1}{2}|(-35) + (-7) + (-18)| = \frac{1}{2}(60) = 30$ units² M1A1

Question 2 — Voronoi Diagrams [6 marks]

(a) $(6, 5.7)$ or $(6, \frac{17}{3})$ (accept $(6, 5.67)$) — where the three perpendicular bisectors meet A1
(b) This point is equidistant from all three warehouses, so it is the farthest possible point from any single warehouse within the triangle. R1R1
Accept "it is equidistant from all three" and "this maximizes the minimum distance to any warehouse".
(c) Midpoint of $W_2W_3$ = $\left(\frac{10+6}{2}, \frac{4+10}{2}\right) = (8, 7)$ M1
Gradient of $W_2W_3$ = $\frac{10-4}{6-10} = \frac{6}{-4} = -\frac{3}{2}$ A1
Perpendicular gradient = $\frac{2}{3}$. Equation: $y - 7 = \frac{2}{3}(x - 8) \Rightarrow 3y - 21 = 2x - 16 \Rightarrow 2x - 3y + 5 = 0$ A1

Question 3 — Pythagoras & Quadratic [6 marks]

(a) Perimeter = $2(2x+5) + 2(x+3) = 6x + 16$ m A1
(b) $(2x+5)^2 + (x+3)^2 = 15^2$ M1
$4x^2 + 20x + 25 + x^2 + 6x + 9 = 225$ A1
$5x^2 + 26x + 34 = 225 \Rightarrow 5x^2 + 26x - 191 = 0$ AG A1
(c) Using GDC: $x = \frac{-26 + \sqrt{676 + 3820}}{10} = \frac{-26 + \sqrt{4496}}{10} = \frac{-26 + 67.05}{10} = 4.105$
$x = 4.11$ (3 sf) or $x = 4.10$ (accept 4.1) M1A1

Question 4 — Cosine Rule & Area [5 marks]

(a) $PR^2 = 8^2 + 10^2 - 2(8)(10)\cos 110° = 64 + 100 - 160(-0.3420...) = 164 + 54.72 = 218.72$ M1
$PR = \sqrt{218.72} = 14.8$ cm (accept 14.79) A1
(b) Area = $\frac{1}{2}(8)(10)\sin 110° = 40 \times 0.9397 = 37.6$ cm² (accept 37.59) M1A1A1
