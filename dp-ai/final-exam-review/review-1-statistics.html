Question 1 — Basic Statistics [5 marks]

(a) $\text{Mean} = \frac{1.85+1.92+1.98+2.01+2.03+2.11}{6} = \frac{11.90}{6} = 1.983\overline{3} \approx 1.98$ m M1A1
(b) $\text{Median} = \frac{1.98+2.01}{2} = 1.995$ m A1
(c) There is no value that appears more than once. No mode (or all values are modes). Accept "no mode" or "no unique mode". A1
(d) $\text{Range} = 2.11 - 1.85 = 0.26$ m A1

Question 2 — Mean from Frequency Table [4 marks]

(a) Total students = $1 + 4 + 7 + 9p + 9 + 4 = 25 + 9p$ A1
(b) Attempt to substitute into mean formula, equating to 4.5: M1
$\frac{1 \times 1 + 2 \times 4 + 3 \times 7 + 4 \times 9p + 5 \times 9 + 6 \times 4}{25 + 9p} = 4.5$
$\frac{1 + 8 + 21 + 36p + 45 + 24}{25 + 9p} = 4.5 \Rightarrow \frac{99 + 36p}{25 + 9p} = 4.5$ A1
$99 + 36p = 112.5 + 40.5p \Rightarrow 4.5p = 13.5 \Rightarrow p = 3$ A1

Question 3 — Box Plots and Outliers [6 marks]

(a) $\text{IQR} = 88 - 62 = 26$ seen anywhere M1
$1.5 \times \text{IQR} = 1.5 \times 26 = 39$ seen anywhere A1
Lower fence = $62 - 39 = 23$. Since $25 > 23$, 25 is not an outlier. AG R1
(b) Supporting: The median score for the evening class (85) is higher than the median for the morning class (79). A1
Countering: The scores are more spread out in the evening class (IQR = 24) than in the morning class (IQR = 26)... wait, let me recalculate. Evening IQR = 92 - 68 = 24. Morning IQR = 88 - 62 = 26. So evening is actually less spread. Better counter: the lowest scores are in the evening class (min = 45 vs 25... actually morning is lower). Hmm.
Correct counter: The interquartile range is lower in the evening class (24 vs 26), suggesting more consistency in the middle 50%. OR the lowest score in the morning class is lower than the lowest in the evening. OR the morning class has a smaller range. A1
Note: If an incorrect comparison is also made, award at most A1A0.

Question 4 — Cumulative Frequency Graph [8 marks]

(a) Median at cumulative frequency 25: approximately 0.30 s (accept 0.28–0.32) A1
(b) Lower quartile at CF 12.5: approximately 0.18 s (accept 0.16–0.20) M1A1
Upper quartile at CF 37.5: approximately 0.42 s (accept 0.40–0.44) A1
$\text{IQR} \approx 0.42 - 0.18 = 0.24$ s (accept 0.20–0.28 following through) A1
(c) 90th percentile at CF 45: approximately 0.70 s (accept 0.66–0.74) M1A1
(d) At $t = 0.4$, cumulative frequency ≈ 18. Number greater than 0.4 = $50 - 18 = 32$ (accept 30–34) M1A1

Question 5 — Grouped Frequency Table [7 marks]

(a) $a = 5$, $b = 5$ (since from graph: CF at 0.2 = 5, and total = 50, so $5+12+15+8+b=50$) A1A1
(b) Modal class: $\mathbf{0.4 \lt t \leq 0.6}$ (highest frequency = 15) A1
(c) Using midpoints (0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 0.9) with frequencies (5, 12, 15, 8, 5):
$\text{Mean} = \frac{5(0.1) + 12(0.3) + 15(0.5) + 8(0.7) + 5(0.9)}{50} = \frac{0.5 + 3.6 + 7.5 + 5.6 + 4.5}{50} = \frac{21.7}{50} = 0.434$ s M1A1
(d) The estimated mean will increase because values are moving from the lowest interval (midpoint 0.1) to a higher interval (midpoint 0.3). R1
The estimated median may increase slightly or stay the same, because moving a few values from the lower end to just above the current median position (which is around 0.30) could shift the median a small amount, but the effect is less pronounced than on the mean. Accept either reasonable argument. R1
