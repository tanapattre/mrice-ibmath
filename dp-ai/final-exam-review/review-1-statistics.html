Final Exam Review 1 — Statistics

Markscheme · 30 marks

Question 1 — Basic Statistics [5 marks]

(a) $\text{Mean} = \frac{2.1+2.3+2.5+2.8+3.0+3.4}{6} = \frac{16.1}{6} = 2.68\overline{3} \approx 2.68$ kg M1A1
(b) $\text{Median} = \frac{2.5+2.8}{2} = 2.65$ kg A1
(c) There is no value that appears more than once. No mode (or all values are modes). Accept "no mode" or "no unique mode". A1
(d) $\text{Range} = 3.4 - 2.1 = 1.3$ kg A1

Question 2 — Mean from Frequency Table [4 marks]

(a) Total students = $4 + 5p + 8 + 6 + 3 + 2 = 23 + 5p$ A1
(b) Attempt to substitute into mean formula, equating to 3: M1
$\frac{1 \times 4 + 2 \times 5p + 3 \times 8 + 4 \times 6 + 5 \times 3 + 6 \times 2}{23 + 5p} = 3$
$\frac{4 + 10p + 24 + 24 + 15 + 12}{23 + 5p} = 3 \Rightarrow \frac{79 + 10p}{23 + 5p} = 3$ A1
$79 + 10p = 69 + 15p \Rightarrow 10 = 5p \Rightarrow p = 2$ A1

Question 3 — Box Plots and Outliers [6 marks]

(a) $\text{IQR} = 72 - 52 = 20$ seen anywhere M1
$1.5 \times \text{IQR} = 1.5 \times 20 = 30$ seen anywhere A1
Lower fence = $52 - 30 = 22$. Since $28 > 22$, 28 is not an outlier. AG R1
(b) Supporting: The median time for the evening session (68 s) is lower than the median for the morning session (61 s), meaning evening swimmers were generally faster. A1
Countering: The times are more spread out in the evening session (IQR = 23 s) than in the morning session (IQR = 20 s), showing more inconsistency. OR the evening session has a lower minimum time (24 s) than the morning session (28 s), meaning the slowest evening swimmer was slower than the slowest morning swimmer. OR the evening session has a larger range (62 s) than the morning session (52 s). A1
Note: If an incorrect comparison is also made, award at most A1A0.

Question 4 — Cumulative Frequency Graph [8 marks]

(a) Median at cumulative frequency 30: approximately 4.8 min (accept 4.6–5.0) A1
(b) Lower quartile at CF 15: approximately 3.0 min (accept 2.8–3.2) M1A1
Upper quartile at CF 45: approximately 6.0 min (accept 5.8–6.2) A1
$\text{IQR} \approx 6.0 - 3.0 = 3.0$ min (accept 2.6–3.4 following through) A1
(c) 90th percentile at CF 54: approximately 7.2 min (accept 7.0–7.4) M1A1
(d) At $t = 4$, cumulative frequency ≈ 24. Number greater than 4 min = $60 - 24 = 36$ (accept 34–38) M1A1

Question 5 — Grouped Frequency Table [7 marks]

(a) From graph: CF at 2 min = 6, so $a = 6$. Total = 60, so $6 + 18 + 12 + 14 + b = 60 \Rightarrow b = 10$. A1A1
(b) Modal class: $\mathbf{2 \lt t \leq 4}$ (highest frequency = 18) A1
(c) Using midpoints (1, 3, 5, 7, 9) with frequencies (6, 18, 12, 14, 10):
$\text{Mean} = \frac{6(1) + 18(3) + 12(5) + 14(7) + 10(9)}{60} = \frac{6 + 54 + 60 + 98 + 90}{60} = \frac{308}{60} = 5.13$ min M1A1
(d) The estimated mean will increase because values are moving from the lowest interval (midpoint 1 min) to a higher interval (midpoint 3 min). R1
The estimated median may increase slightly or stay the same, because moving a few values from the lower end to just above the current median position (around 4.8 min) could shift the median a small amount, but the effect is less pronounced than on the mean. Accept either reasonable argument. R1
