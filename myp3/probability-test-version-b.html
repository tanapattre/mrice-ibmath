Question 1 [6 marks]

A spinner has three equal sections labelled Red, Blue, and Green. The spinner is spun twice.

(a) Draw a tree diagram with two sets of branches to represent this experiment. Label all branches with their probabilities. [3]

(b) Calculate the probability of getting Red on the first spin and Blue on the second spin. [2]

(c) Determine the probability of getting the same colour on both spins. [1]

Question 2 [2 marks]

(a) Write down what it means for two events to be dependent.

(b) A bag contains 4 red and 2 blue marbles. Two marbles are drawn without replacement. State whether the events are dependent. Justify your answer.

Question 3 [2 marks]

The probability that a bus arrives on time is $0.85$.

(a) Calculate the probability that the bus does not arrive on time.

(b) Show that the sum of these two probabilities is equal to 1.

Question 4 [2 marks]

(a) Write down what is meant by the term sample space.

(b) A spinner has four equal sections labelled 1, 2, 3, and 4. List all the outcomes in the sample space.

Question 5 [5 marks]

Two fair four-sided dice (labelled 1, 2, 3, 4) are rolled.

(a) Construct a 2D sample space diagram showing all possible outcomes. Label your axes. [3]

(b) Calculate the probability that the sum of the two dice is greater than 5. [2]

Question 1 — Tree Diagram (Two Events) [6 marks]

(a) First branches: Red ($\frac{1}{3}$), Blue ($\frac{1}{3}$), Green ($\frac{1}{3}$). Second set same. 9 outcomes shown. A1A1A1
(b) $\frac{1}{3} \times \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{9}$ M1A1
(c) $P(RR) + P(BB) + P(GG) = \frac{1}{9} + \frac{1}{9} + \frac{1}{9} = \frac{1}{3}$ A1

Question 2 — Dependent Events [2 marks]

(a) Two events are dependent if the outcome of the first affects the outcome of the second. A1
(b) Yes, dependent. After removing the first marble, fewer remain, so probabilities change. A1

Question 3 — Complementary Events (Bus) [2 marks]

(a) $1 - 0.85 = 0.15$ A1
(b) $0.85 + 0.15 = 1.00$ AG A1

(a) The sample space is the set of all possible outcomes of an experiment. A1
(b) $\{1, 2, 3, 4\}$ A1

Question 5 — 2D Sample Space [5 marks]

(b) Sums > 5: (2,4), (3,3), (3,4), (4,2), (4,3), (4,4) — 6 outcomes. $P = \frac{6}{16} = \frac{3}{8}$ M1A1
